About This Site

Last Updated: 2024-03-20
  • This is a place to host my learning and my collection of learning resources.
  • Goal is to have an extremely high rate of learning. Enough to get me to the top 1% of learners. My interests are in:
    • Game programming
    • Game design
    • Indie game business
  • Projects: Project-based learning section, where I create things from my learning. Projects are based on scientific method: Hypothesis, methods, results, conclusions (see: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0LhmdYa5vvA)
  • Notes: A mind-map like series of notes on various resources. The goal is NOT to summarize and regurgitate, but to create genuine understanding in a "knowledge tree":
    • Concepts are branches
    • Details are leaves
    • Focus on finding connections between information
  • Problems: A section for the most important, absolutely vital "what do I need to solve" questions in my life.
  • Resources: A collection of high-quality material to refer back to later:
    • Essentially the "queue" for future learning
    • Plan: Process one resource per week
    • Convert each into a mind map in the notes section

Personal Observation

I find it curious that high-quality notes (especially of the knowledge tree variety) from extremely smart and high-profile people aren't readily available online. While there are plenty of essays on personal blogs, where are everyone's actual notes and learning process? I'd like to either find them or set an example here (not that I'm claiming to be either smart or high-profile). How do the top 1% in any field learn, and do they all keep their notes private? And if they're public, where are they?
